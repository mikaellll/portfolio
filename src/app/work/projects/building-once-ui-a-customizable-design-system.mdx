# 1. Business Context

Tesla is a global leader in electric vehicles and energy solutions.  
However, in 2025, the company faces **margin compression, rising operating costs, and volatile revenue growth**, requiring stronger management control tools and financial monitoring.

This case study presents a financial analysis and a management control framework supported by Power BI dashboards.

---

# 2. Financial Overview (2025)

## Key Financial Metrics

- **Total Revenue:** ~$94.8B  
- **Operating Margin:** ~4.6% (significant decline vs historical levels)  
- **Cash & Cash Equivalents:** ~$44B  
- **Total Debt:** ~$14.7B  
- **Free Cash Flow:** Positive but declining  

**Key Insight:** Tesla remains solvent and liquid but profitability and growth momentum are deteriorating.

---

# 3. Key Risks Identified (Management Control Perspective)

## Margin Compression  
- Aggressive price cuts and rising production costs reduced operating margins.  
- High fixed costs increase the break-even point.

## Dependence on Regulatory Credits  
- Non-recurring and volatile revenue source.  
- Direct impact on net income and valuation.

## Rising Opex and Capex  
- Heavy investments in AI, robotics, and gigafactories.  
- Increasing fixed cost structure risk.

## Revenue Volatility  
- Quarterly delivery fluctuations impact cash flow predictability and forecasting accuracy.

Provide a high-level financial performance overview for executives and CFO-level decision-making.
